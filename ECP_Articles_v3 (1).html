Quick Rule Reference
A vs AN — Core Rule
A → consonant SOUND
AN → vowel SOUND (a,e,i,o,u)
A university (yoo) · AN hour (silent h)
AN MBA (em) · A European (yoo)
THE — Rule No-5 (Superlative)
THE before superlative adjectives
THE best · THE tallest · THE most · THE greatest
THE — Rule No-10 (MEGA)
THE before major geographical features
THE Ganga · THE Himalayas · THE Pacific
THE Sahara · THE Bay of Bengal
Rule No-6 — Double Comparative
THE more...THE more (both need THE)
The higher we go, the colder we feel
Rule No-18 — Ordinal Numbers
THE before first, second, third...
THE first rank · THE second innings · THE third chapter
Omission — Rule No-6
No article: primary purpose visits
go to school · go to church · go to hospital
THE school/church/hospital = other purpose
Omission — Rule No-2
No article: languages, seasons, sports,
subjects, days, months, festivals, meals
English · cricket · winter · Sunday · Diwali
Omission — Rule No-3
No article: material & abstract nouns
in GENERAL sense
Gold is precious · Wisdom is power
THE gold in this ring (specific) → THE
Silent-H Trap
Silent H = vowel sound → AN
AN hour · AN honest man · AN honour
AN heir · AN honourable → silent H

All Article Rules — From Class Notes
All 28+ rules from your ECP Articles class notes. Tap any section to revise.
Use of A and AN
Rule No-1 Exclamations (What)
What a/an + singular countable noun!
What a hot day! · What a pretty girl!
What an intelligent boy!
Rule No-2 Sense of "Per"
A/AN = per (distributive)
Rs.20 A dozen · Rs.20,000 A month
A hundred · A thousand · A lakh
Rule No-3 Verbs used as Nouns
A/AN before verb-nouns
A walk · A ride · A talk · A swim
He goes for A walk daily.
Rule No-4 Fixed Phrases
A/AN in fixed idiomatic phrases
in a fix · in a nutshell · make a noise
tell a lie · a pity · in a hurry
A vs AN Vowel/Consonant SOUND
AN → vowel sound (a,e,i,o,u)
A → consonant sound
Silent H words → AN
AN attendant/orange/elephant/owl/umbrella
A European/university/union/uniform/unique
AN hour/honest/honourable/heir
Use of THE
Rule No-1 Particular Person/Thing
THE when both speaker and listener know which one
This is THE book I want.
THE pen which you gave me was lost.
Rule No-2 Unique Objects of Nature
THE before unique natural bodies
THE Sun · THE Moon · THE Earth · THE Sky
Rule No-3 Historical Buildings
THE before historical/public buildings
THE Taj Mahal · THE Red Fort
THE Charminar · THE Parliament
Rule No-4 Holy/Popular Books
THE before scriptures and famous books
THE Ramayana · THE Gita · THE Bible
THE Quran · THE Wings of Fire
Rule No-5 Superlative Degree
THE before ALL superlative adjectives
THE greatest · THE richest · THE best
THE most beautiful · THE tallest
Rule No-6 Double Comparative
THE...THE (both must have THE)
THE higher we go THE colder we feel
THE more you talk THE less I understand
Rule No-7 One out of Two
THE + comparative when choosing from two
She is THE cleverer of THE two girls.
It is THE better of THE two options.
Rule No-8 THE + Adjective = Plural Noun
THE + rich/poor/blind/deaf/dumb/sick/dead
THE rich should help THE poor.
A school for THE blind in our town.
Rule No-9 Trains, Ships, Aeroplanes
THE before names of vehicles
THE Rajdhani Express · THE Titanic
THE Jet Airways
Rule No-10 MEGA RULE — Geography
THE before: lakes, rivers, seas, oceans,
mountain ranges, island groups, deserts,
waterfalls, planets, group countries
THE Ganga/Godavari · THE Himalayas
THE Pacific · THE Bay of Bengal
THE Sahara · THE USA/UK/UAE
Rule No-11 Musical Instruments
THE before musical instruments (with 'play')
THE piano · THE violin · THE flute
THE guitar · THE keyboard
Rule No-12 Important Inventions
THE before names of inventions
THE computer · THE television
THE telescope · THE internet
Rule No-13 Newspapers & Magazines
THE before all newspaper/magazine names
THE Hindu · THE Hindustan Times
THE Times of India · THE Eenadu
Rule No-14 Political Parties
THE before all political party names
THE BJP · THE Congress · THE TDP
THE BRS · THE Labour Party
Rule No-15 Historical Events
THE before important historical events
THE First World War
THE Quit India Movement
THE Salt Satyagraha
Rule No-16 Directions
THE before direction names (used geographically)
THE North · THE South · THE East
THE West · THE North-East
Rule No-17 Physical Positions
THE before positional words
THE top · THE bottom · THE left
THE right · THE middle
Rule No-18 Ordinal Numbers
THE before first, second, third, fourth...
THE first rank · THE second innings
THE third chapter · THE fourth attempt
Rule No-19 Nationality Words
THE before nationality (as plural noun)
THE Americans · THE Indians
THE French · THE Japanese · THE Greek
Rule No-20 Committees & Commissions
THE before committee/commission names
THE Srikrishna Committee
THE Kothari Commission
Rule No-21 Singular Noun for Whole Class
THE + singular noun = represents all
THE dog is a faithful animal.
(= All dogs are faithful)
Rule No-22 Designations
THE before designation/post names
THE Prime Minister · THE President
THE Director · THE Principal
Rule No-23 Personality within Person
THE before personality noun (inside someone)
THE judge in him prevailed.
THE mother in her could not see his suffering.
Rule No-24 SAME and WHOLE
THE before 'same' and 'whole'
THE same colour · THE same time
THE whole country · THE whole day
Rule No-25 Major Sport Events
THE before major sporting events
THE World Cup · THE Olympics
THE Asian Games · THE Wimbledon
Rule No-26 Already Mentioned (2nd time)
1st mention = A/AN → 2nd mention = THE
I saw A boy and A girl. THE boy was tall.
Rule No-27 International Organisations
THE before all international organisations
THE UNO · THE WHO · THE ILO
THE UNESCO · THE NATO
Rule No-28 Countries (Plural Names)
THE before countries with plural nouns
THE Netherlands · THE Philippines
THE Maldives · THE United States
Omission of Articles (No Article)
Omission Rule No-1 Proper Nouns
No article before names of persons/places
BUT: proper noun as common noun → THE
Narendra Modi · Hyderabad · Chiranjeevi
Kalidasa is THE Shakespeare of India
Omission Rule No-2 Languages etc.
No article before: languages, diseases,
seasons, games, subjects, days, months,
festivals, meals, plural class nouns
English · cancer · winter · cricket
Sunday · August · Diwali · lunch
Omission Rule No-3 Material/Abstract Nouns
No article: general sense
THE: specific/particular sense
Gold is precious. (general = no article)
THE gold in this ring. (specific = THE)
Omission Rule No-4 Man and Woman
General sense → no article
Particular sense → THE
Man is mortal. (general = no article)
THE man standing there (particular = THE)
Omission Rule No-5 Two Nouns + AND
Same person → one THE only
Different persons → THE before each
THE chairman and MD is on leave. (1 person)
THE chairman and THE MD have visited. (2)
Omission Rule No-6 Primary Purpose Places
No article: primary purpose
THE: other purpose
go to school (study) · go to church (pray)
go to THE school to pay fee (other purpose)
Omission Rule No-7 Fixed Phrases
No article in these fixed expressions
catch fire · by mistake · in danger
at last · to lose heart · set foot
Omission Rule No-8 What kind/sort/type of
No article after: what kind of / type of / sort of
What sort of man he is!
What kind of person she is!
Omission Rule No-9 By + Vehicle
No article between BY and vehicle
by bus · by train · by car
by air · by sea · by road

Core A vs AN Rule

A → consonant SOUND | AN → vowel SOUND

Judge by SOUND not spelling. 'A university' (yoo sound). 'An hour' (silent h). 'An MBA' (em sound). 'A European' (yoo).

THE Key Rules

Specific · Superlative · Unique · Second mention
Ordinals · Same/Whole · Geography

THE before: the best (superlative), the first (ordinal), the Ganga (river), the Himalayas (range), the same, the whole.

No Article Cases

Abstract nouns · Material nouns · Languages
Primary purpose: school/church/hospital/bed

Wisdom, gold, iron, English → no article in general sense. Go to bed/school/church for primary purpose → no article.

Common SSC Error Patterns

  • 'a oculist' → AN oculist (vowel 'o')
  • 'A tallest man' → THE tallest (superlative)
  • 'The honesty' → Honesty (abstract, general)
  • 'more you' → THE more you (double comparative)

Silent H Trap

SILENT H → vowel sound → AN
AN hourAN honestAN honourAN heir
Y-sound: A university, A unique, A European, A useful → consonant sound → A

Fixed Expressions

By air/road/train (no article)
